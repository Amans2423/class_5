1️⃣ How Does a Computer Store Information?

💡 Step-by-Step Story:

Imagine you are writing a story. When you are writing on a notebook placed on your table, it’s easy to work — but if someone takes your notebook away, your work is gone unless you saved it. A computer works the same way.

🔶 What is Information?

Anything you type, draw, or save on a computer is called information. The computer needs a place to store it. That’s where memory comes in!

📦 Two Types of Computer Memory:

  • RAM (Random Access Memory): Temporary working memory. It's very fast, but forgets everything when the computer is turned off.
  • Hard Disk: Permanent storage. It keeps your files safe even after shutdown.

🧪 Real Life Comparison:

  • RAM = Study Table: Books on the table help you study quickly, but they don’t stay there forever.
  • Hard Disk = School Bag: You store your books here to carry and use them later.

🧠 Think Like This:

  • You open MS Paint and start drawing. That drawing is kept in RAM.
  • If you shut down without saving — drawing is lost. ❌
  • When you click SAVE — drawing is sent to Hard Disk. ✅

📊 Memory Comparison Table:

Memory Type Speed Power Off Safe? Used For
RAM (Primary) Very Fast No ❌ Working memory
Hard Disk (Secondary) Slower Yes ✅ Permanent saving

🕹️ Other Storage Devices:

  • Pendrive – small and portable
  • CD – round and shiny disk
  • SSD – super fast storage used in new laptops

🎥 Activity:

Record a video explaining saving a file. Show what happens before and after clicking SAVE.

🎯 Summary:

  • Computer uses RAM to work fast, but it's temporary.
  • It stores information permanently in Hard Disk or similar devices.
  • ALWAYS save your work before turning off the computer.
